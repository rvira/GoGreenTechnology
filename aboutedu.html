Knowledge of electronics and basic programming

We all use electronic appliances every day. These include mobile phones, televisions, bluetooth speakers, drones and electric vehicles. It is necessary for todayˆas generation to know what goes inside making such appliances. Almost each device which we use has an integration of hardware and software components. Software is used to encode the procedure of functioning of the hardware. Students should have a general idea of this concept irrespective of their stream of education. We can see people are moving from labour intensive jobs to automation. There is absolutely no shortage of scope in this domain.


Hands on learning

Practical hands-on learning is a time and tested method of learning, which never goes wrong. And that exactly is the need of the hour. Classroom learning can prove more effective if the students apply the same concepts on actual hardware beforehand. Hands-on learning not only helps students in lifelong learning but the experience gathered in the meantime also gives them a lot of confidence.


Innovation and Creativity

Experimenting with this kit would generate curiosity in them and enable them to think independently. This gives rise to innovative thought processes which help in inventing new products which can make our way of life even easier.

Need for educational robotics

The employment scenario is drastically changing from labour intensive jobs to automation. Students should have a general idea of this concept irrespective of their stream of education. Hence educational robotics is included within the so-called STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics) education as a teaching model in which practice takes precedence over theory. So for us, as aspiring engineers, it’s important to explore this aspect to provide aid for the same and hence we started this development of the EDU-ROBO kit.
